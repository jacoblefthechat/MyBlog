Why the Job Market feels like a Scam

We were promised a golden ticket. Study hard, get the four-year degree, maybe stack a master’s on top of it, and the world is your oyster. But for millions of us, that promise has evaporated, leaving behind a generation that is overqualified, underpaid, and frankly, exhausted.

If you feel like the system is rigged, it’s because it is. We aren’t just fighting for jobs; we’re fighting against an automated, inhuman bureaucracy that treats human ambition like a data problem to be optimized.

The "Entry-Level" Paradox

The most insulting part? The "Entry-Level" label. LinkedIn data shows that over 40% of positions labeled "entry-level" now demand 3+ years of experience. You need a job to get experience, but you need experience to get a job. It’s a closed loop designed to keep new graduates on the outside looking in.

The Ghost in the Machine

The human connection in hiring is dead. When you hit "Apply," your resume goes into an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) that hunts for keywords. If your formatting is slightly off or you miss one niche industry term, you’re rejected before a human even knows you exist. Worse yet, many of the jobs you’re applying to are "ghost jobs"—listings posted by companies that have no intention of hiring, just to project growth or build a talent pool. It’s not just demoralizing; it’s a waste of our time.
